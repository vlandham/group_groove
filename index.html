GroupGroove

Add that bookmarklet to your browser and start sharing the Groove!

A little bookmarklet to turn Grooveshark into a collaborative playlist service.

The basic idea is to combine Grooveshark's broadcasting capabilities with a hack to auto-accept all listener suggestions to transform the broadcast into something truly communal

Setup

One user is both a broadcaster as well as a subscriber (I suggest having 2 separate accounts for this). All other interested parties are subscribers with special privileges.

Broadcaster

  • login to Grooveshark
  • add a few songs to your queue
  • start broadcasting your queue
  • share the broadcast link somewhere public - where others can find it
  • keep your Grooveshark page open to Overview or Suggestions panel
  • run Group Groove bookmarklet on page

Subscribers

  • get link from broadcaster
  • subscribe and start listening to broadcast
  • navigate around and suggest more songs
  • they will be automatically added to the broadcast!
  • voila - collaborative playlists for you and your friends

Shortcomings and Caveats

I would recommend the broadcaster use a separate account for broadcasting and subscribing - which lets you keep the broadcast panel open at the right spot. You can mute the audio from the broadcaster account easily - so you don't get doubled up on songs.

Grooveshark wants to kill broadcasts that aren't actively playing music. I'm not sure what the time limit on this is. But this might limit the reuse of a consistent broadcast link. There might be a way to persist broadcast links in other ways.

Doesn't seem to be a good way to pause the playlist. You are set into autoplay immediately. If you stop, the broadcast is over.

Can be difficult for subscribers to see what songs are coming up. Not sure if there is a way to see this.
